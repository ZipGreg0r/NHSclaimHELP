‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a medical negligence claim take? +
Most claims take 18 months to 2 years if settled out of court. Court cases can take 2-4 years. Your solicitor will keep you updated throughout.
What evidence do I need? +
Medical records, appointment letters, documentation of delays, and evidence of how the delay affected your health. Request your NHS records using the A2H form (Subject Access Request).
How much will it cost me? +
Nothing if you lose. If you win, your solicitor typically takes 25% of the compensation as a success fee. Other costs (expert reports, court fees) are usually covered by insurance.
Can I claim for emotional distress? +
Yes. "General damages" cover physical pain, emotional suffering, anxiety, depression, reduced quality of life, and sleep disturbance caused by the negligence.
What if I missed the 3-year deadline? +
In some circumstances, claims can proceed outside the 3-year window if you have good reason for the delay. Discuss with a solicitor - they can advise on your specific situation.
